## Nadwyżka — founder review

### 1. Speculative physics presented as plausible discovery (high)

The story blends real research (Vienna team's gamma excess / weak pulsar population in *Physical Review Letters*) with a fictional "new interaction field" that fits the simulation when pulsars and dark matter fail. The ending deliberately leaves all three explanations open (dark matter, new field, simulation error).

**Risk:** Readers may treat the fictional field hypothesis as established science.  
**Recommendation:** Confirm trust classification *Inspired by reality* is sufficient, or add a short in-app/editorial disclaimer for this pack.

### 2. Publication timeline compression (medium)

Alarm → alternative simulation → submission → six-month PRL acceptance with global controversy is narratively tight. Real PRL review, replication demands, and institutional pressure typically unfold over longer horizons.

**Risk:** Unrealistic expectations about how fast bold claims propagate.  
**Recommendation:** Accept as dramatic compression, or note in editorial metadata.

### 3. Father / teaching-through-challenges motif (medium)

Tadek's engineer father teaches via challenges, errors-as-steps, and "search for the invisible" — structurally similar to pedagogical themes elsewhere in the library (e.g. Boliewicz-adjacent material about learning through problems rather than memorization).

**Risk:** Unintentional series echo or reader fatigue if Collection Seven expands with the same beat.  
**Recommendation:** Decide whether Collection Seven should share a explicit "teacher lineage" theme or diversify.

### 4. Harvard callback (low)

The Harvard physicist's phone call validates Tadek's courage. It reads as emotional closure, not a verified replication.

**Risk:** Implies external confirmation that the text never substantiates.  
**Recommendation:** Fine for fiction; flag if marketing copy suggests "confirmed by Harvard."

### 5. Grant loss vs. publication success (low)

Tadek loses two grants for "risky" research while the article passes PRL. The trade-off is stated, not explored.

**Risk:** None for comprehension; optional polish if founder wants a sentence on institutional cost.  
**Recommendation:** Optional micro-edit in a future edition — **not applied in Phase 83.**
